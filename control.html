Many sociologists, psychologists, economists, and political scientists agree that protecting academic freedom should be a vital priority for the United States. Our colleges and universities provide many of the creative sparks that stoke the engines of our productivity, and academic freedom sustains the hearts of these institutions. True academic freedom is not contingent on the popularity of a given idea; faculty and students must be free to question, study, speak, and create even when their ideas and conclusions run counter to majority beliefs. This is fundamental to American ideals. In stressful times especially, we must make every effort to hold in our minds the complexity of what we say we believe. The primary purpose of our universities is preserving and advancing our understanding of life, the world, and the universe -- of discovering truth. If the U.S. is to maximize its human potential and remain a leader among nations, such discovery is crucial.
